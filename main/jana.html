    Madagascar, in many ways, can be considered as one of the last sanctuary of wildlife on the planet. Its biodiversity is famous for being endemic at 90%. But the “Great Island” has also been a human melting pot for thousands of year and is today the result of a big mixture. A mixture of beliefs, of cultures, languages and know-how from Asia, Africa and Europe.

    • Locations

    The safari journey will take you to some places in Madagascar. Outside Asia, Madagascar has the longest history of rice production. Wetland rice production systems are well developed and rice terraces are frequently found along the roads between Antananarivo and Antsirabe. The Isalo National Park is a protected area of Madagascar which is declared a national park in 1962. Tsingy Bemaraha is an impressive spiny mountain - amazing landscape of limestone. Saint Marie, Ifaty Anakao will offer you an opportunity for snorkeling and diving. In the morning, you can see the local people from their fishing trip. Baobab Avenue has huge trees as roots on the sky and very famous for sunset over these endemic trees. Of the nine species of baobabs registered in the world, seven of them are endemic to Madagascar.
